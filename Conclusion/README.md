## Final Words of Wisdom

### Remember These Principles:

**1. Simple Before Complex**
> "Make everything as simple as possible, but not simpler." â€” Einstein

Start with linear regression. If it works well enough, stop there. Complexity should be justified by significant improvement.

**2. Data is King**
> "Better data beats fancier algorithms."

Spend time on data quality, feature engineering, and understanding your data. This has higher ROI than algorithm tweaking.

**3. Measure What Matters**
> "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." â€” Goodhart's Law

Optimize for business metrics, not just ML metrics. Understand what you're really trying to achieve.

**4. Iterate Intelligently**
> "Perfect is the enemy of good."

Ship something that works, measure its impact, and improve iteratively. Don't wait for perfection.

**5. Learn Continuously**
> "The only constant in ML is change."

New techniques emerge constantly. Stay curious, read papers, try new things. But don't chase every trend.

**6. Communicate Clearly**
> "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."

Being able to explain ML to non-technical stakeholders is as important as building models.
